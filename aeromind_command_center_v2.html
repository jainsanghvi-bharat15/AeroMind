FY 2024 Flight Operations — AeroMind Analysis: Across 500,000 operations spanning 348 airports and 15 carriers, average departure delay settled at 12.5 minutes, with 36.7% of flights experiencing some delay. Cancellation rate held at a relatively controlled 1.36% (6,811 flights), suggesting operational disruptions are delay-driven rather than cancellation-driven.

Seasonal Risk: July recorded the highest average delay at 22.6 min, followed by May (18.3) and June (16.4) — a clear summer convective weather pattern. October and November performed best at under 6.5 min average, indicating Q4 operational stability. Q2 and Q3 are the highest-risk operational quarters.

Carrier Performance Gap: American Airlines (AA) averaged 20.5 min delay versus YX at just 4.2 min — a 16-minute spread across the same network. Delta (DL) and Alaska (AS) outperform peers substantially.

Recommendation: Prioritize buffer-scheduling intervention at ASE, JAC, MIA, and DFW. A targeted delay-reduction program at these four airports could recover an estimated ~18–22% of total delay minutes given their disproportionate risk scores relative to flight volume.
